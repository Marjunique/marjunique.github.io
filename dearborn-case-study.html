City of Dearborn | Aug - Dec 2023

Optimizing Translation Process

Enhancing translation services for city announcements and civic information to better serve Dearborn's Arabic-speaking community.

Role

Lead Design Researcher

Timeline

5 months (Aug - Dec 2023)

Team

3 researchers + City staff

Introduction

The City of Dearborn, serving a diverse community where nearly 30% of the population speaks Arabic at home, identified a need to enhance translation services for City announcements and civic information, particularly on the city website.

My team and I were working to answer the following question: How can the City of Dearborn optimize the translation of English content using Google English Translation HUB (ETH) to enhance communication with the entire community?

Understanding the Present Workflow

I began by mapping the current translation process to identify pain points across different stakeholders.

Current Process Steps

  1. 1. Internal/External departments submit translation request
  2. 2. Document submitter receives document and inputs into Google ETH
  3. 3. Document is submitted to Google Translation Hub
  4. 4. Document is translated by Google ETH
  5. 5. Translation is reviewed by internal Arabic-speaking employees
  6. 6. Revised translation is shared with requesting department

Key Pain Points

We identified several key pain points within the translation process. These include three of our target audiences that would be directly impacted by the translation process each at different parts of the process.

Staff Translation Challenges

  • • Lack of centralized process for translation requests
  • • Dependency on Arabic-speaking staff volunteers
  • • Extra workload for bilingual employees

Resident Translation Challenges

  • • Language barriers limiting access to city information
  • • Translation accessibility issues
  • • Lack of awareness about available translation services

Google ETH Limitations

  • • Trust and privacy concerns with confidential documents
  • • Troubleshooting challenges and lack of support from Google representatives
  • • Inaccurate translations requiring extensive manual revision
  • • Limited access to additional features

By understanding the challenges faced by each audience, we tailored our solutions to address their specific needs and improve the overall effectiveness of the translation workflow.

Solutions

Short-Term Solutions

1. Workflow Management System

Implement a centralized system to streamline translation requests and tracking.

Key Features:

  • • Translation request form for standardized submissions
  • • City staff receives and tracks requests
  • • Status tracking and change management
  • • File version history
  • • Owner assignment capabilities

2. Invest More Into Google ETH

Subscribe to Google ETH plan that allows users to continuously update and have those edits updated in the Glossary as well.

Benefits:

  • • Improved translation accuracy over time
  • • Custom glossary for city-specific terminology
  • • Reduced manual revision workload

Long-Term Solutions

Translation Team

Establish and compensate multiple translation teams, including a full-time and community volunteer translation team.

Civic Engagement

Encourage civic engagement amongst community members by facilitating more opportunities to give feedback on translated documents.

Impact

Our research has had a significant impact on the communications department. By sharing our findings with their superiors, the process to obtain translation vendors has been expedited.

This ensures the involvement of external, professional translators to verify the accuracy of translations. As a result, Arabic-speaking employees are relieved of this responsibility, allowing them to focus on their primary duties.
