Project Goal

Develop AI-powered translations that ensure consistency, use program-specific terminology, and provide clear translations for Spanish and Haitian Creole-speaking communities accessing New Jersey's Temporary Disability Insurance (TDI) and Family Leave Insurance (FLI) benefits.

Current Challenges

  • • No standardized bilingual glossary
  • • Complex terms hard to translate clearly
  • • Spanish translations lag weeks behind English
  • • Haitian Creole quality perceived variably

Intended Outcomes

  • • Validated 3-language glossary
  • • Translation prompt library
  • • Scalable usage guidelines
  • • Framework for expansion

My Contribution

As Project Manager and UX Researcher, I led the end-to-end development of an AI-powered translation system for the NJ Department of Labor's Temporary Disability and Family Leave programs.

  • • Led mixed-methods research including 1:1 interviews with 5 Spanish-speaking participants and group interviews with Haitian Creole speakers
  • • Developed and refined 20+ Haitian Creole glossary terms and plain language guidelines for English content
  • • Collaborated with community-based organizations and NJDOL staff to ensure cultural and linguistic accuracy
  • • Created AI translation prompts and evaluation rubrics that resulted in translations outperforming vendor quality
  • • Validated translation quality with native speakers across three languages

Phase 1: Plain English Glossary Development

Methods: Content analysis, plain language evaluation

Reviewed 40+ technical terms and rewrote for clarity using PlainLanguage.gov checklist. Updated existing glossary to meet accessibility standards, ensuring 6th-8th grade reading level, active voice, and contextualized definitions within the user journey of qualifying for benefits.

Key Activities:

  • • Applied plain language principles: direct address, short sentences, common words
  • • Added contextual information about deadlines and eligibility criteria
  • • Tested readability to ensure 6th-8th grade comprehension level

Phase 2: AI Translation Prompt Development

Methods: Iterative prompt engineering, stakeholder feedback sessions

Developed language-specific prompts that incorporated translation guidelines, cultural nuances, and program-specific terminology. Refined prompts based on feedback from community organizations and NJDOL staff to ensure accuracy, cultural sensitivity, and consistency.

Key Activities:

  • • Created prompts incorporating gender neutrality for Spanish
  • • Minimized French interference for Haitian Creole translations
  • • Aligned with W3C accessibility standards

Phase 3: Spanish Community Testing (n=5)

Methods: 1:1 comparative interviews, translation quality assessment

Conducted 1:1 interviews comparing AI-generated vs human translations of 12 glossary definitions. Participants reviewed randomized comparison decks to reduce bias, rated difficulty of understanding on 1-5 scale, and provided preference ratings and qualitative feedback.

Key Activities:

  • • Randomized A/B presentation to eliminate bias
  • • Collected both quantitative ratings (1-5 scale) and qualitative feedback
  • • Identified patterns in comprehension across technical terms

Phase 4: Haitian Creole Community Testing

Methods: Focus groups, translation quality assessment

Conducted group interviews comparing Google Translate vs AI vs human translations of 8 glossary definitions. Participants ranked three versions (A, B, C) for each term, discussed contextual appropriateness and cultural relevance, and identified terms with strong legal or formal connotations.

Key Activities:

  • • Group discussion format to surface cultural nuances
  • • Identified words requiring contextual explanations (e.g., "claim")
  • • Evaluated appropriateness of conversational vs. formal tone

Additional Insight: When preferences existed, human translations were selected twice as often as AI, but participants were confused by technical terms and acronyms in both versions.

Impact

Established a scalable, AI-powered translation framework that enables NJDOL to provide faster, more consistent, and culturally appropriate translations for Spanish and Haitian Creole-speaking communities accessing TDI/FLI benefits.

Key Outcomes

  • • AI translations outperformed vendor translations in accuracy and cultural relevance
  • • Validated glossaries for plain English, Spanish, and Haitian Creole (20+ terms refined)
  • • Reduced translation lag time from weeks to days by enabling AI-assisted translations with human review
  • • Standardized glossary ensures consistent terminology across all TDI/FLI communications
  • • Created replicable model for expanding to additional languages and benefit programs
  • • Community-validated approach builds trust with native speakers and community organizations
