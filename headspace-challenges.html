Headspace | May 2023 - August 2023

Gamifying Mindfulness: Headspace Challenges

Leading mixed-methods research to transform challenges from an email-based B2B offering into an integrated in-app gamification system, developing a 3-phase strategy to increase engagement and expand to D2C markets.

My Contribution

As Service Design Intern and UX Researcher, I led research initiatives that informed a comprehensive product strategy for Headspace's challenges feature, working with a cross-functional team of 7 interns.

  • Conducted mixed-methods research including survey research (n=30), stakeholder interviews with B2B clients and internal teams, and competitive analysis of gamification approaches in wellness apps
  • Synthesized findings into a 3-phase product roadmap prioritizing customization & storytelling, achievement & progression, and community connectedness
  • Identified four evidence-based design principles for encouraging challenge participation: psychoeducation, rewards & affirmations, community building, and habit formation
  • Collaborated with brand designers to develop "The 3 C's" visual design system (Community, Challenges, Celebration) with custom illustrations and color palette
  • Created email templates, in-app UI concepts, push notification strategy, and social media content to support phased rollout

Project Goal

Explore B2B challenges and the current beta test to identify areas of improvement and ways to increase employee engagement. Additionally, identify if Headspace should expand challenges to a D2C offering.

Current Challenges

  • Lack of personalization — challenges were one-size-fits-all, not tailored to individual user needs or preferences
  • Social loafing — in group challenges, individuals contributed less effort because they assumed others would pick up the slack
  • Lack of seamless in-app integration — challenges were managed via email outside the app, creating friction and reducing engagement

Intended Outcomes

  • Evidence-based strategy for improving challenges based on user research and competitive analysis
  • Design system and visual language for challenges that aligns with Headspace brand
  • Phased roadmap for scaling challenges from B2B email to in-app D2C experience
  • Framework for measuring engagement, retention, and business impact

Process & Methods

Phase 1: Discovery Research

Methods: Stakeholder interviews, literature review, competitive analysis

Conducted interviews with B2B stakeholders, enterprise admins, engineering teams, and client success managers to understand business needs, technical constraints, and current challenges performance. Reviewed scientific research on gamification, motivation theory, and group dynamics. Mapped competitive landscape from static to dynamic challenges across wellness and care positioning.

Key Activities:

  • Interviewed 5+ stakeholders including Gigi Fioravanti (Enterprise Admin), Ryan Routon and Alex Baranov (Engineering), Anne Robertson (Client Success), and B2B Challenges Team
  • Analyzed competitors including Calm, Wellable, InsightTimer, BurnAlong, SuperBetter, and Habitica
  • Reviewed academic research on gamification effectiveness (Nike Run Club study showing XX% engagement increase)

Phase 2: User Research

Methods: Survey research, preference testing

Conducted "Express Mission: Are you up for a challenge?" survey with 30 Headspace members to understand preferences for team dynamics, motivation factors, incentives, and social aspects of challenges.

Key Activities:

  • Surveyed 30 members on individual vs. group preferences, competition attitudes, and motivating factors
  • Tested preferences for achievements/badges, charitable donations, and leaderboard competition
  • Assessed comfort levels with discussing meditation and mindfulness with others
  • Gathered feedback on potential challenge types (individual goal-based, small group goals, large group collective)

Phase 3: Strategy Development & Design

Methods: Synthesis workshops, design system creation, roadmap planning

Synthesized research findings into strategic recommendations, collaborating with brand designers to create visual design system and with product managers to develop phased implementation roadmap. Created deliverables including email templates, in-app UI concepts, and content guidelines.

Key Activities:

  • Developed 3-phase product roadmap (Build Infrastructure → In-App MVP → App-Wide Integration)
  • Created "The 3 C's" design system with custom color palette (oranges, yellows, blues)
  • Designed 5 email templates, in-app modal concepts, push notifications, and social media templates
  • Defined metrics for measuring B2B contract renewals and D2C engagement/churn reduction

Key Insight: Studies show that gamification increases user engagement when there are elements of customization & meaningful storytelling, achievement & progression, and connectedness with other users working toward common goals.

User Preferences on Team Dynamics

Members prefer working individually when joining a challenge

When given a choice between individual or group challenges, members prefer working independently to maintain autonomy and avoid social loafing.

If they have to be part of a team, members prefer working with people familiar to them

Members prefer teaming up with friends, family, or colleagues they know for challenges rather than being placed in a random group.

User Preferences on Motivation & Incentives

Achievements/badges and charitable donations are universally motivating

In both company-wide and Headspace-led challenges, members would be motivated to participate with these incentives.

Competition was polarizing

While some members loved the idea of competing and thought it would help them build a habit, some members disliked gamification of mindfulness and meditation.

Members feel comfortable talking to friends, family, or coworkers about meditation & mindfulness

Community discussion could be a valuable component to challenges.

Design Principles for Encouraging Participation

Based on research and literature review, identified four key principles to cultivate intrinsic motivation in challenges:

  • Highlight Psychoeducation: Communicate the health benefits of participation to participants
  • Rewards and Affirmations: Include rewards that affirm participants to continue their positive progress
  • Foster Community: Create a sense of community with a common goal that emphasizes the importance of mental health
  • Promote Habit Building: Encourage the creation of habit through reminders
