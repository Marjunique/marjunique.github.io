Searchable Study Spaces Booking System

University of Michigan Library — 2023

Role

UX Researcher

Timeline

7 months (Oct 2022 - Apr 2023)

Methods

Competitive analysis, Usability testing, Data analysis

Project Goal

Improve the library's study space reservation system with searchable and filterable features to make spaces findable for students.

Students experienced issues searching and booking study spaces on the library's booking site, often unable to find spaces that matched their needs for noise level, atmosphere, and amenities.

My Contribution

As UX Researcher, I led the usability testing phase of this collaborative research project, conducting tests across multiple university booking systems to understand how students interact with different interface patterns.

  • • Conducted competitive analysis of 4 university booking systems (Cambridge, NYU, UT Austin, Duke)
  • • Led 15 usability tests with U of M students
  • • Analyzed filter categories, functionality, and search-ability across different systems
  • • Tested mock-ups of vertical and horizontal filter layouts
  • • Synthesized qualitative feedback into actionable themes and recommendations

Process

1. Competitive Analysis

Analyzed booking systems at University of Cambridge, NYU, UT Austin, and Duke to compare filter categories, filter functionality, search-ability, atmosphere descriptions, location metadata, and features.

2. Mock-up Development

Collaborated with UX designer who created two mockups of the study space booking website with filter values for "Noise level" and "Atmosphere." Tested both vertical and horizontal versions.

3. Usability Testing

Conducted 15 usability tests, carefully optimizing task order and combinations to prevent bias and data manipulation. Tested across multiple university systems to understand student preferences.

4. Data Synthesis

Identified key themes and highlighted outliers across all testing sessions to support design decisions for the future reservation booking system.

Findings

1. Visual Information is Critical

Students emphasized the importance of images and descriptions to understand space characteristics before booking. They wanted to see what the space looked like and understand its atmosphere, not just know its technical specifications.

2. Students Need Navigation Support

Students needed directions and guided instructions throughout the booking process. The current systems assumed familiarity with library spaces and booking procedures that new users didn't have.

3. Filtering Out Non-Reservable Spaces

Students were frustrated when they found spaces that couldn't actually be reserved. They were eager for a feature that would filter out non-reservable spaces from results, saving time and reducing confusion.

4. Noise Level Indicators Highly Requested

Indicators for noise levels were highly requested to help students find appropriate study environments. Students noted that noise level was often their primary decision factor when choosing a space.

Impact

Research findings provided clear direction for feature prioritization and design decisions for the future reservation booking system.

Key Outcomes

  • • Developed recommendations for robust filtering system including noise level, atmosphere, group size, and amenities
  • • Identified need for visual context (photos and descriptions) for each space
  • • Established requirements for guided navigation and tooltips for first-time users
  • • Provided framework for standardizing space metadata across all library locations
