Empathizing with Users through UX Research

Exceptional Lives — 2022

Role

UX Researcher

Timeline

3 months (Aug - Oct 2022)

Methods

User scenarios, Survey design, Workshop facilitation

Project Goal

Test the Exceptional Lives website taxonomy and foster empathy within the team by encouraging them to experience the site as users would—families navigating special education resources.

Current Challenges

  • • Team didn't understand user mental models
  • • Site cluttered with too much information
  • • Lacked visual hierarchy
  • • Confusing for new users to find resources

Intended Outcomes

  • • Test website taxonomy with team
  • • Build team empathy for users
  • • Identify navigation barriers
  • • Inform content strategy

My Contribution

As UX Researcher, I designed and facilitated a research process that turned internal team members into users, generating insights and empathy that drove organizational change.

  • • Developed survey with user scenarios and tasks to prompt team empathy
  • • Deployed survey to 9 team members (CEO, content team, marketing team)
  • • Facilitated one-hour group workshop to discuss experiences and findings
  • • Synthesized insights that informed website redesign and content strategy
  • • Created research methodology that became model for ongoing Family Feedback Team research

Phase 1: Survey Development

Methods: Scenario design, task creation

Created scenarios simulating real parent journeys through the website, asking team members to find specific resources as if they were families in need. This prompted them to experience the site without their insider knowledge.

Key Activities:

  • • Designed realistic user scenarios based on common family needs
  • • Created tasks requiring navigation through multiple site sections
  • • Built survey using Google Forms for easy deployment

Phase 2: Survey Deployment

Methods: Individual completion, independent testing

Distributed the survey to nine team members, giving each 15-20 minutes to complete it independently before the group discussion. This ensured honest, unbiased feedback.

Key Activities:

  • • Sent survey to CEO, content team, and marketing team
  • • Collected responses independently before workshop
  • • Prepared synthesis of initial themes

Phase 3: Workshop Facilitation

Methods: Group discussion, collaborative synthesis

Led a one-hour group workshop to discuss experiences and findings. This session revealed how the team's internal knowledge had masked usability issues glaringly obvious to new users.

Key Activities:

  • • Facilitated discussion of team members' experiences
  • • Identified patterns across different departments
  • • Built shared understanding of user challenges

Phase 4: Synthesis & Recommendations

Methods: Thematic analysis, actionable recommendations

Synthesized team insights into actionable recommendations that informed content strategy, website redesign, and ongoing research practices.

Key Activities:

  • • Identified key themes from survey and workshop
  • • Developed recommendations for content and navigation
  • • Created framework for ongoing family research

Findings

4

Critical Usability Issues

That the team's insider knowledge had been masking from view

1

Too Many Options on Landing Page

The website presented overwhelming choices without clear hierarchy or guidance. New users didn't know where to start.

"Too many options on landing page, I didn't know where to start."

2

Excessive Clicking Required

Information was buried deep in the site structure, requiring multiple clicks to find basic resources.

"I found helpful info immediately, and with each click there was more. A LOT... didn't know where to click next, but just chose links that stood out for my personal purposes."

3

Location-Based vs. Topic-Based Navigation

The menu prioritized geographic organization over topical searches, misaligning with how parents actually look for help.

"We have a very location based focus in the top menu, more than topical for our main resources. I'm not likely to come to the site to look for a medium, I'm coming with a topic/question."

4

Team Recognition of Barriers

The exercise successfully created empathy—team members recognized they had made navigation unnecessarily difficult.

"Wow, do we ever make it hard for people to find what they need!"

Impact

The research methodology was so effective that it became the model for ongoing research with our Family Feedback Team, establishing a scalable approach to gathering user insights.

Key Outcomes

  • • Survey methodology became standard for ongoing Family Feedback Team research
  • • Findings informed complete overhaul of content development strategy, prioritizing topic-based organization
  • • Research process opened channels for ongoing collaboration with families, creating continuous feedback loop
  • • Validated need for user-friendly resources, resulting in additional funding
  • • Drove comprehensive website redesign focusing on clear navigation and visual hierarchy
