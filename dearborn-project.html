Optimizing Translation Process

City of Dearborn — 2023

Role

Lead Design Researcher

Timeline

5 months (Aug - Dec 2023)

Methods

Stakeholder interviews, Process mapping, Service design

Project Goal

Optimize the translation of English content using Google English Translation HUB (ETH) to enhance communication with Dearborn's Arabic-speaking community, which represents nearly 30% of the population.

Current Challenges

  • • Lack of centralized process
  • • Volunteer dependency on Arabic-speaking staff
  • • Google ETH limitations impact quality
  • • Residents face multiple access barriers

Intended Outcomes

  • • Map complete translation workflow
  • • Identify systemic pain points
  • • Develop short and long-term solutions
  • • Reduce burden on staff volunteers

My Contribution

As Lead Design Researcher, I mapped the end-to-end translation workflow and identified pain points across three distinct user groups: city staff, Arabic-speaking residents, and Google Translation Hub administrators.

  • • Conducted stakeholder interviews with city staff, Arabic-speaking residents, and Google Translation Hub users
  • • Mapped 6-stage translation workflow from request to distribution
  • • Identified pain points for three user groups: staff, residents, and Google ETH users
  • • Developed short-term and long-term recommendations
  • • Presented findings to city leadership, expediting procurement of professional translation vendors

Phase 1: Translation Request

Methods: Stakeholder interviews, process observation

Examined how internal and external departments submitted translation requests. Documented the lack of centralized process creating inconsistency and lack of visibility across departments.

Key Activities:

  • • Interviewed department staff about current request processes
  • • Mapped informal and formal request channels
  • • Identified gaps in process standardization

Phase 2: Document Received & Submitted to Google ETH

Methods: User interviews, tool evaluation

Analyzed how documents moved through the system, identifying trust and privacy concerns when handling confidential documents. Evaluated troubleshooting issues users faced due to lack of support from Google representatives.

Key Activities:

  • • Documented privacy concerns with sensitive city documents
  • • Identified Google ETH support gaps and troubleshooting challenges
  • • Assessed limitations of free-tier translation features

Phase 3: Document Translated & Revision

Methods: Translation quality assessment, staff interviews

Evaluated machine translation quality and documented how internal Arabic-speaking employees volunteered to review translations, creating unsustainable workload and potential conflicts of interest.

Key Activities:

  • • Assessed accuracy of machine translations
  • • Documented volunteer workload and stress on Arabic-speaking staff
  • • Identified conflicts of interest when staff reviewed their own department materials

Phase 4: Revised Translation Shared

Methods: Resident interviews, accessibility evaluation

Explored how final translations reached residents and identified lack of feedback mechanisms for community input. Many residents were unaware that translated materials existed.

Key Activities:

  • • Interviewed Arabic-speaking residents about translation awareness
  • • Documented translation delays (weeks behind English updates)
  • • Identified need for community feedback mechanism

Findings

5

Critical Pain Points Identified

Across three user groups affecting 30% of Dearborn's population

1

Workflow Lacks Centralization

Without a standardized process, translation requests were handled inconsistently across departments, causing delays and duplicate work. Staff lacked visibility into request status.

2

Volunteer Model Creates Unsustainable Burden

Arabic-speaking staff were volunteering translation time on top of primary job responsibilities, creating stress and potential conflicts of interest when reviewing materials from their own departments.

3

Google ETH Limitations Impact Quality

Machine translations required extensive manual revision. Limited support from Google and privacy concerns with confidential documents constrained effectiveness. Free tier limited access to quality-improving features.

4

Residents Face Multiple Access Barriers

Residents were often unaware that translated materials existed or how to access them. Translation delays meant Arabic content was weeks behind English updates, creating inequitable access to time-sensitive information.

5

No Feedback Mechanism

Residents lacked opportunities to provide feedback on translation quality or cultural appropriateness, missing chances for community empowerment and continuous improvement.

Impact

Research findings had immediate impact on the communications department. By presenting data-backed insights to city leadership, the process to obtain professional translation vendors was expedited.

Key Outcomes

  • • City expedited procurement of external, professional translators to verify translation accuracy
  • • Arabic-speaking employees relieved of volunteer translation responsibilities, allowing focus on primary duties
  • • Framework established for more consistent and timely translation of city communications
  • • Workflow management recommendations provide foundation for sustainable translation operations serving 30% of Dearborn's population
