City of Dearborn | Aug - Dec 2023

Optimizing Translation Process

Enhancing translation services for city announcements and civic information to better serve Dearborn's Arabic-speaking community, representing nearly 30% of the population.

My Contribution

As Lead Design Researcher, I led a comprehensive research project to optimize the City of Dearborn's translation workflow for serving its diverse Arabic-speaking community.

•

Conducted stakeholder interviews with city staff, Arabic-speaking residents, and Google Translation Hub users to identify pain points across the translation workflow

•

Mapped the end-to-end translation process, identifying friction points at each stage for three distinct user groups

•

Synthesized research findings into actionable short-term and long-term recommendations

•

Project Goal

Optimize the translation of English content using Google English Translation HUB (ETH) to enhance communication with Dearborn's Arabic-speaking community, which represents nearly 30% of the population.

Context

The City of Dearborn identified a critical need to enhance translation services for city announcements and civic information, particularly on the city website. The existing workflow using Google Translation Hub faced several challenges that limited accessibility and increased burden on staff.

Research Question

"How can the City of Dearborn optimize the translation of English content using Google English Translation HUB (ETH) to enhance communication with the entire community?"

Process & Methods

Phase 1: Understanding the Current Workflow

Methods: Process mapping, stakeholder interviews, workflow analysis

Mapped the end-to-end translation workflow to understand how translation requests moved through the system, from initial submission by internal and external departments to final distribution.

Current Process Identified (6 Steps):

1. Internal/External departments submit translation request

2. Document submitter receives document and inputs into Google ETH

3. Document is submitted to Google Translation Hub

4. Document is translated by Google ETH

5. Translation is reviewed by internal Arabic-speaking employees

6. Revised translation is shared with requesting department

Phase 2: Identifying Pain Points

Methods: Stakeholder interviews, user journey mapping, thematic analysis

Conducted interviews with three key stakeholder groups to understand challenges at different stages of the translation process.

Staff Pain Points

• Lack of centralized process for translation requests

• Dependency on Arabic-speaking staff volunteers

• Extra workload for bilingual employees beyond their primary duties

Resident Pain Points

• Language barriers limiting access to city information

• Translation accessibility issues on city platforms

• Lack of awareness about available translation services

Google ETH Limitations

• Trust and privacy concerns with confidential documents

• Troubleshooting challenges and lack of support from Google representatives

• Inaccurate translations requiring extensive manual revision

• Limited access to additional features

Phase 3: Developing Solutions

Methods: Solution ideation workshops, feasibility assessment, stakeholder validation

Synthesized research findings into actionable recommendations, categorizing solutions by implementation timeline and impact. Collaborated with city leadership to prioritize solutions based on feasibility and community benefit.

Findings

Key Insights

1. Translation Workflow Lacks Centralization

Without a standardized process, translation requests were handled inconsistently across departments. Staff lacked visibility into request status, causing delays and duplicate work. This affected both internal efficiency and external service delivery to residents.

2. Volunteer Model Creates Unsustainable Burden

Arabic-speaking staff were volunteering translation time on top of their primary job responsibilities. This created stress, potential conflicts of interest when translating materials from their own departments, and inconsistent availability for translation needs.

3. Google ETH Limitations Impact Quality

While Google Translation Hub provided a starting point, machine translations required extensive manual revision. Limited support from Google representatives and privacy concerns with confidential documents further constrained the tool's effectiveness.

4. Residents Face Multiple Access Barriers

Beyond language barriers, residents were often unaware that translated materials existed or how to access them. Translation delays meant Arabic content was often weeks behind English updates, creating inequitable access to time-sensitive information.

Recommendations Developed

Short-Term Solutions

Workflow Management System

Implement centralized system with translation request forms, status tracking, version history, and owner assignment capabilities

Invest in Google ETH

Subscribe to advanced plan allowing continuous updates and custom glossary development for city-specific terminology

Long-Term Solutions

Professional Translation Team

Establish and compensate dedicated translation teams, including full-time staff and compensated community volunteers

Civic Engagement Program

Create opportunities for community members to provide feedback on translated documents, ensuring cultural and linguistic accuracy

Impact

Research findings had a significant and immediate impact on the communications department. By presenting data-backed insights to city leadership, the process to obtain professional translation vendors was expedited.
