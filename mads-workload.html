MADS Workload Data Collection Investigation

University of Michigan — 2022

Role

UX Research Consultant

Timeline

5 months (Aug - Dec 2022)

Methods

Contextual inquiry, Stakeholder interviews, Data analysis

Project Goal

Investigate workload data collection issues and develop actionable recommendations for improvement.

The Master of Applied Data Science (MADS) program needed to better understand how course workload data was being collected, processed, and used across different departments to improve data collection for better course planning and student experience.

My Contribution

As part of the EyesOnYou Team, I conducted contextual inquiry to understand MADS' workload data collection process, the Course Operations Team, and their connection to instructors.

  • • Conducted background research on existing processes and documentation
  • • Led 6 stakeholder interviews with faculty and staff across departments
  • • Facilitated contextual inquiry sessions observing actual workflows
  • • Built affinity walls for pattern identification across departments
  • • Mapped communication flows between stakeholders
  • • Developed multi-step system recommendations for standardizing data collection

Process

1. Background Research

Conducted individual background research on the MADS program structure, course operations, and existing data collection methods. Collaborated to create comprehensive interview protocol, testing and refining it for gathering insights.

2. Contextual Inquiry

Conducted 6 interviews with faculty and staff from various departments—Course Operations, Academic Affairs, and Instructional teams—to understand communication patterns and identify gaps not visible in documentation.

3. Affinity Wall Building

Used affinity wall building to synthesize findings and identify patterns across different departments and roles. Revealed systemic issues that weren't obvious from individual interviews.

4. Communication Flow Mapping

Mapped current communication flow between stakeholders and developed ideal vision showing where transparency was lacking and how to address gaps.

Findings

1. Departmental Transparency Gap

Significant lack of transparency between stakeholders about how workload data was being collected, processed, and used. Different departments had incomplete pictures of the overall process.

2. Automation Without Follow-up

While systems were automated for instructors to submit workload data, there was insufficient follow-up to ensure instructors understood how to use them or had their questions answered.

3. Inconsistent Terminology

Different departments used varying terminology for the same concepts. What Course Operations called "workload estimates" was "time investment data" to Academic Affairs and "course hours" to instructors.

4. One-Way Communication

Communication flowed from Course Operations to instructors with no feedback loop on data completeness or quality. Unclear ownership of follow-up when instructors didn't respond.

Impact

Developed multi-step system to standardize data collection, disbursement, and utilization with focus on transparency, communication, regular surveys, and debriefs to create continuous improvement cycle.

Key Outcomes

  • • Recommended shared data dictionary to establish standardized terminology across all departments
  • • Proposed regular instructor check-ins after data submission deadlines to address confusion and ensure quality
  • • Developed framework for cross-departmental visibility through quarterly meetings
  • • Designed feedback loop providing instructors summary reports on workload estimates vs. actual student time
  • • Created modular implementation approach allowing incremental progress rather than complete overhaul
