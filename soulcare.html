Soul Care: Culturally Responsive Mental Health for Foster Youth

Public Policy Lab for NYC Administration for Children's Services — 2024

Role

Timeline

4 months (May - Aug 2024)

Methods

Project Goal

Develop culturally responsive mental health and wellness support for young people in foster care by creating a holistic framework and guidebook that helps youth coaches guide participants through their wellness journeys.

Current Challenges

  • • Youth primarily black and brown experiencing complex trauma
  • • Treatment relationships end prematurely
  • • Lack of culturally competent providers
  • • Eurocentric therapeutic approaches not resonating

Intended Outcomes

  • • Create holistic well-being framework
  • • Develop coach guidebook and tools
  • • Structure youth engagement model
  • • Pilot program across 5 agencies

Phase 1: Research & Discovery

Methods: Literature review, stakeholder interviews

Conducted research on holistic wellness models and interviewed psychology professionals to understand evidence-based frameworks. Reviewed existing therapeutic approaches and identified gaps in culturally responsive care for black and brown youth.

Key Activities:

  • • Interviewed psychology professionals about holistic wellness frameworks
  • • Researched culturally responsive therapeutic approaches
  • • Identified dimensions of wellness relevant to foster youth experiences

Phase 2: Framework Development

Methods: Co-creation, iterative design

Developed the Soul Care Holistic Well-Being Framework (also known as the "Soul Care Solar System") that helps youth explore different dimensions of life contributing to mental health and wellness. Created framework to be youth-friendly, visual, and accessible.

Key Activities:

  • • Designed holistic framework encompassing multiple wellness dimensions
  • • Created visual representation accessible to youth
  • • Developed accompanying educational materials for coaches

Phase 3: Guidebook Theme & Structure

Methods: Content design, narrative development

Created the thematic structure and narrative approach for "The Soul Care Guide to Well-being" that coaches would use as an educational tool. Ensured content was trauma-informed, empowering, and culturally relevant.

Key Activities:

  • • Developed guidebook structure and thematic organization
  • • Created narrative tone emphasizing youth agency and choice
  • • Designed content to support coaches in facilitating wellness conversations

Phase 4: Youth Engagement Structure

Methods: Interviews with youth and coaches

Interviewed youth coaches, advocates, and youth to understand how to structure meaningful peer engagement. Categorized different types of engagement activities and developed framework for group experiences.

Key Activities:

  • • Conducted 6+ interviews with coaches, advocates, and youth
  • • Categorized types of peer engagement activities
  • • Developed structure for agency-based group experiences

Findings

5

Key Needs Identified

For young people in foster care seeking mental health support

1

Choice, Agency, and Dignity

Youth need autonomy in their mental health journeys, not prescriptive solutions. They want to be active participants in decisions about their care, not passive recipients.

"For me, they have to make therapy not feel like therapy."

2

Consistent Culturally Competent Relationships

Youth need stable relationships with providers who understand their cultural backgrounds and lived experiences, not constantly changing case workers.

"When I was 7 or 8, my case planners would change like every other month...I loved that woman. I had seen her for a couple of months, and then she was gone."

3

Peer Support from Similar Experiences

Youth benefit from connecting with peers facing similar life situations. Group experiences and peer support reduce isolation and build community.

4

Access to Alternative Wellness Activities

Youth ranked arts participation and individual exercise as most impactful activities—higher than traditional therapy. They need earlier interventions through creative and physical wellness activities.

"Asking the foster parents what would help is a lot more helpful than the agency deciding this kid needs therapy, right? A foster parent might say, 'actually, I think what this kid needs is music lessons.'"

5

Skills Development Support

Youth need support developing self-reflection, communication, and emotional regulation skills through guided practice and consistent coaching relationships.

Impact

The Soul Care Holistic Well-Being Framework and guidebook became foundational tools in a comprehensive program piloted across five foster care agencies serving approximately 900 youth across New York City.

Key Outcomes

  • • Created holistic framework (Soul Care Solar System) adopted across NYC foster care system for youth wellness planning
  • • Developed guidebook enabling Fair Futures coaches to facilitate trauma-informed wellness conversations
  • • Structured peer engagement model providing blueprint for agency-based group experiences
  • • Program piloted across 5 agencies with plans for citywide scaling by 2026
  • • Framework emphasized youth choice and agency, moving away from prescriptive therapy models
  • • Connected youth with alternative wellness providers including hip-hop therapy, kickboxing, and performance-based practice
